Why I Chose AI Engineering as My Career Path

December 20, 2025 ‚Ä¢ 5 min read

Choosing a career path is one of the most important decisions we make. When I decided to pursue AI engineering at MCT Howest Kortrijk, it wasn't a random choice ‚Äì it was the culmination of experiences, interests, and a vision for the future I want to help create.

The Spark

My interest in AI began when I first encountered a recommendation algorithm that seemed to understand my preferences better than I did myself. How could a machine predict what I'd enjoy? This simple question led me down a rabbit hole of machine learning, neural networks, and artificial intelligence.

I started watching YouTube videos about AI, reading articles, and trying small coding projects. The more I learned, the more fascinated I became. AI wasn't just about algorithms ‚Äì it was about creating systems that could learn, adapt, and solve problems in ways that were previously impossible.

The Real-World Impact

What really solidified my decision was seeing AI's potential to make a meaningful difference in people's lives:

  • Healthcare: AI diagnosing diseases earlier and more accurately than traditional methods
  • Accessibility: Computer vision helping visually impaired people navigate the world
  • Climate: ML models optimizing energy consumption and predicting environmental changes
  • Education: Personalized learning systems adapting to each student's needs

These weren't just theoretical possibilities ‚Äì they were already happening. I wanted to be part of this transformation.

Why MCT Howest Kortrijk?

Choosing the right program was crucial. MCT Howest stood out for several reasons:

1. Practical Approach

The program emphasizes hands-on learning. It's not just theory ‚Äì we build real projects, work with actual datasets, and solve practical problems. This learning style fits me perfectly.

2. Comprehensive Curriculum

The curriculum covers everything from fundamental mathematics and programming to advanced deep learning and deployment. It provides both breadth and depth.

3. Industry Connections

Howest has strong connections with tech companies, providing opportunities for internships and real-world projects. This bridge between education and industry is invaluable.

4. Research Opportunities

The program encourages research and innovation. Having the opportunity to work on cutting-edge AI problems excites me.

The Intersection of My Interests

AI engineering perfectly combines several of my passions:

Problem-Solving

I've always loved puzzles and challenges. AI engineering is essentially solving complex problems ‚Äì how to make machines learn, generalize, and make intelligent decisions.

Mathematics and Logic

The mathematical foundations of AI ‚Äì linear algebra, calculus, probability ‚Äì fascinate me. Understanding how elegant mathematical concepts translate into powerful algorithms is deeply satisfying.

Creativity

Many people think engineering is purely logical, but AI requires tremendous creativity. Designing neural network architectures, feature engineering, and problem formulation all require creative thinking.

Sports and Human Performance

My interest in soccer and physiotherapy isn't separate from my AI career ‚Äì it's complementary. AI applications in sports analytics and injury prevention represent a perfect blend of my interests.

The Challenges I Expected

I went into this field with my eyes open to the challenges:

Rapid Change

AI evolves incredibly fast. What's cutting-edge today might be outdated in six months. This requires continuous learning, which I see as exciting rather than daunting.

Mathematical Complexity

Understanding the mathematics behind AI algorithms requires significant effort. I knew this would be challenging but also rewarding.

Ethical Considerations

AI has tremendous power, which comes with responsibility. Issues like bias, privacy, and fairness require careful consideration. I'm committed to developing AI ethically.

High Competition

AI is a hot field, attracting talented people worldwide. Standing out requires dedication, unique perspectives, and continuous improvement.

My Goals in AI

I have specific goals that drive my studies:

Short-term (During Studies)

  • Master deep learning fundamentals
  • Build a portfolio of meaningful projects
  • Contribute to open-source AI projects
  • Publish research or technical blog posts
  • Complete an impactful internship

Medium-term (First 5 Years)

  • Work on AI applications in healthcare or sports
  • Develop expertise in a specialized AI domain
  • Mentor aspiring AI engineers
  • Contribute to making AI more accessible

Long-term Vision

I want to work on AI systems that genuinely improve people's everyday lives. Whether that's helping athletes prevent injuries, assisting doctors in diagnoses, or creating tools that make technology more accessible ‚Äì I want my work to have positive impact.

The Support System

No one succeeds alone. I'm fortunate to have:

  • Supportive family who believe in my goals
  • Passionate professors who inspire and challenge me
  • Fellow students who share knowledge and motivation
  • Online communities where I can learn and contribute

Staying Motivated

Motivation isn't constant ‚Äì it fluctuates. Here's what keeps me going:

  • Building projects: Seeing my code work and create something useful
  • Learning new concepts: The "aha!" moments when complex topics click
  • Community engagement: Sharing knowledge and helping others learn
  • Tracking progress: Looking back at what I've accomplished
  • Real-world applications: Reading about AI solving actual problems

Advice for Others Considering AI

If you're thinking about pursuing AI engineering:

  • Start experimenting: Try building something, even if it's simple
  • Don't wait for perfection: You don't need to know everything before starting
  • Find your niche: AI is broad ‚Äì find what excites you specifically
  • Build in public: Share your learning journey
  • Be patient: Mastery takes time
  • Connect with others: The AI community is welcoming and helpful

Looking Forward

I'm still at the beginning of my AI engineering journey, but I'm more convinced than ever that I made the right choice. Every day brings new challenges and learning opportunities. The field is evolving rapidly, and I'm excited to grow with it.

AI engineering isn't just about building intelligent machines ‚Äì it's about augmenting human capabilities, solving complex problems, and creating a better future. That's a mission worth dedicating my career to.
